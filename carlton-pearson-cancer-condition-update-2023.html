Carlton Pearson Health Over the Years

Bishop Carlton Pearson’s life and health journey epitomizes resilience in the face of personal and theological challenges.

As a prominent figure in American religious circles, his life has been marked by significant milestones and controversies.

Raised within the conservative Church of God in Christ, he established the Higher Dimensions Evangelistic Center in Tulsa in 1981, rapidly growing from a modest 75 members to a thriving congregation of over 5,000.

Pearson’s influence extended beyond the church, as he launched the Azusa Conferences at Oral Roberts University in the late 1980s, solidifying his presence in Christian television.

However, a pivotal moment in his life and ministry came when he challenged the conventional concept of hell.

Advocating for inclusionism, a doctrine that promotes universal salvation without the need for repentance or faith in Jesus Christ, Pearson held beliefs that diverged from the mainstream.

As a result, the minister faced ostracization by the Joint College of African-American Pentecostal Bishops Congress in 2004, which branded him a heretic due to his unorthodox theological stance.

Pearson’s controversial beliefs, acknowledging the Bible’s teachings about Hell but questioning its infallibility, led to a transformation in his ministry and personal life.

In his recent health update, he shared the toll his illness had taken on his physical well-being, leading to a substantial weight loss, with his current weight at 137 pounds.

Despite facing challenges, he remains hopeful and expresses gratitude for the global support from his supporters, who have shown love, support, and messages during his trying times.
