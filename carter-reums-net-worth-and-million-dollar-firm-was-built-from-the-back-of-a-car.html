Carter Reums net worth and million-dollar firm was built from the back of a car Thu 30

Publish date: 2024-09-22

Paris Hilton and Carter Reum are parents of two these days. Hilton’s new reality TV show, Paris in Love, kicks off season 2 this November. The two become a mom and dad for the first time during the new show. Paris says her life is “finally complete” after accumulating a huge net worth in her life, and now starting a family.

After knowing one another for a decade, Carter Reum and Paris Hilton got together in 2019 and later tied the knot in 2021. Now, their life together is shown in her Peacock reality series which begins season 2 on November 30.

Carter Reum’s net worth

As a former investment banker and businessman, Carter has built up an estimated net worth of $20 million, writes Celebrity Net Worth.

However, Finty reports he was born into a “wealthy family” and has a net worth closer to $40 million.

Per his M13 company website, Carter explains he and his brother, Courtney, previously worked as investment bankers at Goldman Sachs.

The brothers co-founded a liquor company called VEEV, which they later sold in 2016.

Paris is worth even more

A net worth of $20-$40 million is huge, but Carter’s wife, Paris, has an estimated net worth of almost 10 times his.

Celebrity Net Worth estimates the Paris in Love star’s net worth to be at $300 million.

Paris is known for being an heiress and socialite. Her great-granddaughter of Conrad Hilton, the founder of the Hilton Hotel, and her mother is Real Housewives of Beverly Hills star Kathy Hilton.

When Kim Kardashian first landed on the scene, she was Paris’ assistant and friend. Now, Kim has an eye-watering net worth of $1.7 billion.

Paris’ husband built business from his car

Speaking about his career on his M13 website, Carter explained he and his brother co-founded VEEV “out the back of their car.”

He added when they sold the company they were “in every Walmart, Target, and TGI Fridays…”

The brothers’ M13 company is an angel investment firm. The two also co-authored a book, Shortcut Your Startup: Ten Ways to Speed Up Entrepreneurial Success, back in 2018.
