What is the Fastest KO in UFC History?

Publish date: 2024-07-06

The Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC) has witnessed numerous memorable moments throughout its history, including lightning-fast knockouts that have left audiences in awe. 

Defining the Fastest Knockout

The fastest knockout in UFC history is a record that signifies the shortest duration it took for a fighter to win a match by delivering a knockout blow to their opponent. This record serves as a testament to the sheer power, speed, and precision of the fighters involved.

The Five-Second Knockout: Jorge Masvidal vs. Ben Askren 

The record for the fastest knockout in UFC history is currently held by Jorge Masvidal, who achieved this remarkable feat in a bout against Ben Askren at UFC 239.

Masvidal accomplished this extraordinary victory by executing a leaping right knee that connected with Askren's head just as he attempted a takedown, resulting in a lightning-fast knockout that took only five seconds.

Notable Contenders for the Fastest Knockout Title

While Masvidal's five-second knockout stands as the current record, there have been several other notable contenders in UFC history who achieved incredibly swift knockouts.

Some of these memorable knockouts include the seven-second knockout by Todd Duffee, the nine-second knockout by Gray Maynard, and the 11-second knockout by Duane Ludwig.
