How Much Does Saquon Barkley Squat?

Publish date: 2024-07-03

Penn State's former running back, Barkley, was the second overall pick by the New York Giants in the 2018 NFL Draft. His NFL career has accomplished numerous feats, including winning the 2018 Offensive Rookie of the Year and making three Pro Bowl appearances. Part of what makes Barkley an elite running back in the NFL is his workouts, a regimen that he has always maintained. His lower body strength and devotion to weight training, especially heavy leg work at the squat racks, have set him miles ahead regarding physical buildup. These attributes would not have been possible if he had followed the minimum requirements to succeed in his training.

Unveiling the Squat Exercises

One of the most impressive feats demonstrates his incredible lower body strength, used as a squat routine among Barkley's repertoire of breaks. His power, strength, and control, as seen when broadcasting across social media platforms, such as squatting an outstanding amount of weight, will stand him in good stead.

585-Lb Personal Best Squat

Impressive Feats of Strength

Barkley boasts incredible raw power, as evidenced in a video of him squatting an eye-popping 585 pounds—quite the feat for his lower body and size. There is also a video of him completing four reps with this weight barefoot that exemplifies how powerful and well-rounded his training abilities are. This solidifies him among the most fierce in strength, which reigns largely over these lands.

Records and Achievements

Barkley's 585-pound squat is a personal record and an impressive one for any NFLer. This remarkable strength has surely added to one of the first things that may come to mind when you think of Saquon Barkley: Powerhouse.
