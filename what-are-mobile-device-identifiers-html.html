What are mobile device identifiers?

Publish date: 2024-08-04

Answer

A Mobile Device ID is a one-of-a-kind customer identification that is used to identify between different mobile devices. It might be an IDFA (Identifier for Advertisers) or an Android Ad Id, to name a few possibilities.

Is the device ID the same as the IMEI?

Your IMEI is essentially the same as the vehicle identification number (VIN). Your MEID serves as both a personal device identification number and a meta data identifier. It is the number of characters in each identification number that distinguishes the two types of numbers. IMEI numbers are used by GSM networks such as AT&T and T-Mobile.

What does the ID of a gadget look like?

When used on iOS, a device ID is referred to as the ‘Identity for Advertisers’ (IDFA, or IFA for short). The GPS ADID is used as the device ID on Android devices (or Google Play Services ID for Android). User’s GPS ADID may be found in the Google Ads settings menu under ‘Google – Ads,’ where they can also reset the ID and opt-out of ad personalisation.

What is the device ID on an Android phone or tablet?

A unique alphanumeric number is produced for your Android phone when it is initially set up. This code is known as the android device ID. This code effectively serves the same purpose as an IMEI number in that it identifies your device. Android device ID, on the other hand, is especially designed to be used for identifying reasons rather than for monitoring your device.
