Matthew Brennan Cassel, born on May 17, 1982, is a professional American football quarterback who currently plays for the Minnesota Vikings of the NFL. He attended the University of Southern California (USC) and was selected by the New England Patriots in the seventh round of the 2005 NFL Draft. Following a season-ending knee injury to then reigning NFL MVP Tom Brady, Cassel became the Patriots’ starting quarterback from Week 1 of the 2008 season. Notably, he holds the distinction of being the only known quarterback in NFL history to start an NFL game without having started at quarterback during college.

In February 2009, the Patriots awarded Cassel a one-year franchise tag contract worth over $14 million, the largest one-year contract for an offensive player in NFL history. Following this, Cassel was traded to the Kansas City Chiefs, who signed him to a 6-year, $62.7 million contract in July 2009. During his time with the Chiefs, he led the team to their first divisional championship in seven years and earned a Pro Bowl berth in 2010.
