Who is Darius Garland: Biography, Net Worth &amp; more

Publish date: 2024-08-16

Darius Garland is an American professional basketball player for the Cleveland Cavaliers of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played college basketball for the Vanderbilt Commodores before he was selected by the Cavaliers in the first round, 5th overall pick of the 2019 NBA draft. He has so far achieved a few milestones, including NBA All-Star, McDonald’s All-American and three times Tennessee Mr. Basketball.

Who is Darius Garland?

Darius Kinnard Garland was born on January 26, 2000 in Gary, Indiana, United States. He is the last born child of six children birthed to former NBA player Winston Kinnard Garland and Felicia Garland. His siblings are sister Kacie, and brothers Desmond, Kody, Miguel, and Hilton.

At a young age, he began playing baseball and basketball when he turned five. Not long did he turn his sole attention to basketball. He played basketball in elementary school, scoring his first basket in second grade.

Garland attended Brentwood Academy High School in Brentwood, Tennessee. He joined their Varsity basketball team in eighth grade and left as a four-time state champion and three-time Tennessee Mr. Basketball winner.

He committed to playing basketball for Vanderbilt Commodores men’s team. Darius joined the team as the best recruit in program history but a meniscus injury after five games cut short his college career. He left the team in wake to prepare for the NBA draft.

In the 2019 NBA draft, Darius and 76 others were invited to take part in the NBA Draft Combine. On May 15, he left with the notion of being promised a selection by a team in the NBA draft lottery.

Garland was selected by Cleveland Cavaliers in the first round, fifth overall pick in the 2019 NBA draft. He made his debut in a loss against Orlando Magic on October 23, 2019, scoring eight points, two rebounds, five assists and a steal that night.

After mak ing his debut, he made history by becoming the first player born in the 2000s to make his NBA debut. Garland finished his rookie season with an average of 12.3 points and 3.9 assists per game.

On December 23, 2020, Garland made his season debut for the Cavaliers, recording 22 points, six rebounds and six assists in a 121–114 win over the Charlotte Hornets. On April 5, 2021, he scored a then career-high 37 points, along with seven assists in a 125–101 win over the San Antonio Spurs. Despite the Cavaliers missing the playoffs for a third straight season, Garland averaged 17.4 points per game, alongside 6.1 assists and 1.2 steals.
