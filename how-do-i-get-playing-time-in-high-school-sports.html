How do I get playing time in high school sports?

Publish date: 2024-09-16

Getting playing time in high school sports is not always easy, and it can be frustrating when you are not getting the opportunity to showcase your skills. However, there are certain strategies and habits you can adopt to increase your chances of getting playing time.

  • Show up early and work hard

    • One of the most important things you can do as an athlete is to show up early to practice and work hard. This sends a message to your coach that you are committed and serious about the sport. Arriving early also gives you the opportunity to warm up and practice on your own, which can help you improve your skills and stand out during team practices.

  • Be coachable

    • Coaches are looking for athletes who are coachable, meaning they are willing to listen to feedback and make adjustments to their game. If your coach gives you feedback, take it seriously and work on improving in the areas they point out. This shows your coach that you are open to learning and willing to put in the effort to become a better player.

  • Develop a positive attitude

    • Your attitude can have a big impact on your playing time. Coaches want to work with athletes who have a positive attitude and are willing to support their teammates. Avoid complaining or being negative, and instead focus on being a positive force on the team. This can help you build strong relationships with your coaches and teammates, which can lead to more playing time.

  • Be versatile

    • If you are able to play multiple positions, you may increase your chances of getting playing time. Coaches often need to shift players around to compensate for injuries or other issues, and having players who can play multiple positions can be a huge asset. If you are able to play multiple positions, make sure your coach knows and sees you practicing in those positions.

  • Be a good teammate

    • Being a good teammate can also increase your chances of getting playing time. Coaches want athletes who are supportive of their teammates, whether they are on the field or on the bench. Make sure you are cheering on your teammates during games and practices, and be willing to help out in any way possible.

  • Communicate with your coach

    • If you are not getting the playing time you want, it is important to communicate with your coach. Ask for feedback on what you can do to improve and ask what you can do to earn more playing time. Be respectful and open to what your coach has to say, and be willing to put in the effort to make the necessary changes.

  • Work on your weaknesses

    • Finally, if you are not getting playing time, take the opportunity to work on your weaknesses. Focus on improving your skills and getting better in areas where you may be struggling. If you are able to make significant improvements, your coach may take notice and give you more playing time.

    In conclusion, getting playing time in high school sports requires hard work, a positive attitude, and a willingness to be a good teammate. By following these tips, you can increase your chances of getting the playing time you want while also improving as an athlete. Remember, success in sports requires commitment and dedication, so stay focused and keep working hard.
